Why's it important to set a goal?

Goals drive behavior. Goals help you establish priorities. They get people to focus on the right things to the exclusion of others. A clear set of personal goals helps you make meaningful progress. These goals are stepping stones toward your own growth and success, while also contributing to broader outcomes. Following a thoughtful and intentional goal-setting process is one of the most powerful ways to guide your actions and create meaningful change in your life. The right goals help shape the habits, decisions, and behaviors that move you toward the future you want to build. Your personal goals can come from many different areas of life. You might set goals around your career and work performance, but you can—and should—also create goals for your health and fitness, your relationships, how you eat, your role as a parent, your education, your hobbies, your emotional well-being, and the kind of lifestyle you want to lead. These goals are yours. You set them based on your own values, priorities, and the vision you have for your future—not someone else’s agenda. The key is to have enough goals to give your life direction and momentum, but not so many that you feel overwhelmed or scattered. As you define your goals, think about what really matters to you and what kind of person you want to become—and then break that vision down into a set of focused, manageable goals that you can work on over time.
